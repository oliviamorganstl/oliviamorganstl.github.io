First off, as of December 2022 when I am making this website, the class gets you credit for both the Letters and the Civ 2 GE credit, so that's nice.

Also in this class you are responsible for filling out a daily reading log discussing one thing you thought was interesting from that days readings. Shown below are some of the readings we learned about. In class, we discuss with other students how these authors are effective and/or what they could have done better. Throughout the semester, there are a few digital dialogs, a few quizzes, a couple presentations, as well as a final project. This website is an example of what my final project was.

With all assignments, Professor Slater does not give you busy work, he does not spring anything new on you, values your time for other classes, and the assignments require you to use your opinion and not just memorize and repeat loads of facts.

Logic
Nancy Christiansen

Intended as a resource for anyone who is interested in learning more about the principles and techniques of logical reasoning. Created a step by step guide for how to analyze rhetoric for the other works that we read.

Read more

The Education Of Children
Erasmus De Pueris Instituendis

Argues that the primary purpose of education is to teach children how to use their own reason and understand the world around them. The book advocates for a more hands-on, experiential approach to learning, rather than rote memorization and drilling.

Read more

The Book of the Courtier
Baldesar Castiglione

Written as a conversation between several fictional characters who discuss the ideal qualities and behavior of a courtier, or member of the royal court. Their focus remains on courtly life, personal demeanor, conversation, and military service.

Read more

The Art of Rhetoric
Thomas Wilson

Provides guidance on how to use language effectively in a variety of contexts, from public speaking to writing. It also explores the role of the audience in the communication process and the importance of understanding their perspective.

Read more

Paradise Regained
John Milton

Retells the story of Jesus being tempted in the desert by satan. The poem emphasizes that victory comes from the strength of our minds and spirits. Jesus resists the devil's temptations by using reason and spiritual fortitude, ultimately triumphing over evil.

Read more

The Rhetoric of Rhetoric
Wayne Booth

Explores persuasion and its role in effective communication. It discusses logical, emotional, and ethical appeals, and how to use them effectively. Also shows the importance of understanding the audience's perspective and using language that is clear and concise.

Read more

The Secret Garden
Frances Burnett

Tells the story of a young girl who is sent to live in England after her parents die. On the estate, Mary discovers a hidden garden and begins to tend to it while finding a sense of purpose, learning about friendship, and discovering the power of nature to heal.
